# BinKeeper — Marketing Plan
*Handoff context for website and social media development*

---

## Brand Positioning

**Tagline:** Your Hive. Organized.

**One-liner:** BinKeeper turns any bin, box, or container into a searchable inventory — with a QR code and your phone.

**Brand identity:** Bee / honeycomb theme. Amber, warm tones. Organized, friendly, slightly playful. Not clinical or corporate.

**Core promise:** Stop guessing what's in there. Start finding it.

---

## Target Audiences

### Primary: The Household Organizer
- Suburban homeowner, 30–55
- Has a basement, garage, attic, or storage unit with bins they can't see into
- Motivated by seasonal events (holiday prep, spring cleaning, moving)
- Platforms: Facebook, Instagram, Pinterest
- Message: "Finally know what's in every bin."

### Secondary: The Group Gear Manager
- Coach, team parent, club organizer, friend group
- Responsible for shared equipment that moves between people
- Motivated by the "who has the thing?" problem
- Platforms: Facebook Groups, Nextdoor, Instagram
- Message: "Always know who has the gear."

### Tertiary: The Mover
- Anyone in the process of moving — highly time-limited, high urgency
- Motivated by chaos avoidance
- Platforms: Google Search (high intent), TikTok/Instagram (inspiration)
- Message: "Label every box before the truck arrives."

### Opportunistic: Community & Public Bin Operators
- Little Free Library stewards, community pantry managers, church supply coordinators
- Small but highly viral — every public QR code is a BinKeeper ad
- Platforms: Nextdoor, local Facebook Groups, community newsletters
- Message: "Let anyone see what's inside — no app needed."

## Channel Strategy

### 1. App Store Optimization (ASO) — Priority 1, Zero Cost
Do this before spending a dollar on ads. This is your highest-ROI marketing activity at launch.

**Target keywords:**
- home inventory app
- storage bin organizer
- QR bin tracker
- moving box inventory
- household storage app
- bin label app

**App Store listing must include:**
- Screenshots showing the core "scan → find" loop
- Screenshots of the public QR feature (Little Free Library angle sells itself visually)
- Screenshots of group sharing / check-out feature
- Clear pricing mention ("Free to start") in description
- BinKeeper Move called out explicitly for movers

**Review strategy:** Ask your beta users (friends, family) to leave reviews immediately at launch. 10 genuine 5-star reviews in week 1 dramatically improves App Store ranking.

---

### 2. Organic Social — Priority 2, Low Cost / High Time

**Primary platform: TikTok and Instagram Reels**

"Satisfying organization" content performs extremely well organically. You don't need a big following — you need good content and consistency.

**Content pillars:**

**Pillar 1: Before/After**
- Film a chaotic basement or storage room → show it organized with BinKeeper QR codes on every bin
- These are the highest-performing format in the organization niche
- You don't need a studio — your own basement is authentic and relatable

**Pillar 2: The "Who Has It" Problem**
- Short skits: "Me texting everyone on the team asking who has the cones"
- Cut to: BinKeeper check-out log showing exactly who checked out the cones
- Relatable humor drives shares

**Pillar 3: Use Case Spotlights**
- "5 things you didn't know you could use BinKeeper for"
- Little Free Library, poker night, vacation home, moving boxes, youth sports
- Each one surfaces a new audience

**Pillar 4: Moving Content**
- "How I labeled every box before my move" — strong search traffic around moving season (spring and fall)
- BinKeeper Move is a natural star here

**Pillar 5: Community / Public Bins**
- Show someone scanning a Little Free Library QR code and seeing the book list
- Film-able in a real community setting — very shareable

**Posting cadence:** 3–4x per week minimum to build algorithmic traction. Consistency matters more than production quality in early stages.

---

### 3. Nextdoor & Local Facebook Groups — Priority 3, Zero Cost

The group sharing and public bin use cases have natural homes here.

**Tactics:**
- Post in neighborhood groups about the tool-lending or Little Free Library use case
- Offer to help a local community org (church, scout troop, food pantry) set up BinKeeper for free in exchange for a testimonial and post
- These communities are tight-knit — one enthusiastic post from a trusted neighbor drives installs

---

### 4. Paid Social — Priority 4, After Organic Validation

Do not run paid ads until you have:
- At least 25 App Store reviews
- Organic content that has proven to resonate (something with 10K+ views)
- A clear sense of which use case converts best

**When you do run ads:**
- Platform: Meta (Facebook + Instagram) to start
- Targeting: Homeowners, 30–55, interests in home organization, moving, storage
- Creative: Use your best-performing organic content as ad creative (this is called "dark posting" — it's more authentic than polished ad creative)
- Budget: Start at $10–15/day, test 2–3 creative variations, kill losers fast
- Goal metric: Cost per install (CPI) — target under $3.00

**Moving season targeting:**
- Run BinKeeper Move ads in March–May and August–October (peak moving seasons)
- Target people who have recently searched for movers, moving supplies, or real estate listings (Meta has this data)

---

### 5. Google Search — Priority 5, For Mover Acquisition

High-intent searchers looking for "moving box inventory app" or "how to label moving boxes" are your best Move tier customers.

- Run Search ads against: "moving box label app," "inventory moving boxes," "track moving boxes app"
- Budget: Very small to start ($5–10/day) — search volume for these terms is low but intent is very high
- Landing page: A dedicated BinKeeper Move landing page on binkeeperapp.com

## Website (binkeeperapp.com) Content Strategy

The website has two jobs: convert visitors to installs and explain the product to skeptics.

**Must-have sections:**
1. Hero — core promise + app store download buttons
2. How it works — 3 steps: Label, Catalog, Find
3. Use cases — carousel or tabs: Household, Groups, Moving, Community
4. Public QR demo — show what a public bin looks like to someone without the app
5. Pricing — all 4 tiers clearly laid out including Move
6. Social proof — testimonials, review count, download count (add as they grow)
7. Moving-specific section — BinKeeper Move deserves its own callout

**SEO content to create over time:**
- "How to organize your storage bins" (targets home org searchers)
- "Best moving box inventory apps" (targets movers)
- "How to manage shared sports equipment" (targets coaches/team parents)
- "Little Free Library ideas" (opportunistic — high search volume, unique angle)

## Launch Sequence

**Pre-launch (now):**
- [ ] Finalize binkeeperapp.com with email capture
- [ ] Build a list of 20–30 beta users (friends, family, Little League parents, EvenKeel network)
- [ ] Set up social accounts (TikTok, Instagram) — post 2–3 teaser videos before launch

**Launch week:**
- [ ] Submit to App Store
- [ ] Email beta list with download link and review request
- [ ] Post launch announcement across all channels
- [ ] Reach out to 3–5 local community orgs (Little Free Library, church pantry, scout troop) to be early public-bin showcase partners
- [ ] Post in relevant subreddits: r/organization, r/malelivingspace, r/homeimprovement, r/moving

**First 90 days:**
- [ ] Maintain 3–4x/week social posting cadence
- [ ] Collect 5–10 testimonials from beta users
- [ ] Track which use case drives most installs — double down on that
- [ ] Add testimonials and social proof to website
- [ ] Begin light paid social if organic is showing traction

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target (90 days) |
|---|---|
| App installs | 500+ |
| Free → paid conversion | 3–5% |
| Paying users | 15–25 |
| Monthly revenue | $75–150 |
| App Store rating | 4.5+ |
| Social followers (combined) | 500+ |

These are conservative, organic-only targets. They represent break-even validation — proof that people want the product before spending on paid acquisition.
